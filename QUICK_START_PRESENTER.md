## Presentation Dos and Don'ts

### ‚úÖ DO:
- Start with grandmother story (emotional hook)
- Use real pilot numbers (credibility)
- Make eye contact with all judges
- Pause after key points
- Show passion (you care about this)
- Acknowledge challenges honestly
- End with clear ask

### ‚ùå DON'T:
- Use jargon (RAG, Lambda, DynamoDB) unless asked
- Say "revolutionary" or "game-changing" (clich√©)
- Overpromise ("will solve all problems")
- Read slides word-for-word
- Rush through (nervous talking fast)
- Ignore judges' body language
- Forget to say "thank you"

## Body Language Tips

### Voice:
- Start slow and clear
- Speed up slightly during solution (shows excitement)
- Slow down for key numbers (let them sink in)
- Pause after "There is no online for her" (dramatic impact)

### Gestures:
- Open palms when describing problem (inclusive)
- Hands close when describing solution (simple, contained)
- Expansive gesture for vision (big future)
- Hand on heart when saying "dignity" (authentic emotion)

### Eye Contact:
- Start with judge in center
- Rotate to each judge during pitch
- Hold eye contact on key statements
- Look back to center for conclusion

## Q&A Strategy

### If you know the answer:
"Great question. [Answer concisely in 30-60 seconds]"

### If you're not sure:
"That's something we're actively working on. My current thinking is [give your best guess], but I'd love to discuss this further after the presentation."

### If it's in your docs:
"Yes, I've detailed this in my submission. The short answer is [brief summary]. Happy to share the full analysis."

### If they challenge you:
"I appreciate that concern. Here's how we're addressing it: [mitigation strategy]"

**Never:**
- Make up an answer
- Get defensive
- Say "I don't know" without offering anything
- Argue with judges
